Introduction

These are some ideas for actions that can be done at demonstrations or at other times. Some ideas are more related to direct action and others are more playful.  They may require some preparation to create costumes, props, and learn how to improvise and role-play in the group of people who are performing. It's also helpful to invite other people along to film, pass out flyers and talk with the audience during or after the action. Friends can also play part of the audience, because when one person stops to stare, it starts a precedent and kind of gives permission for others to stop and stare, too. Trial and error and adapting to the place and situation is vital to make any action successful.
